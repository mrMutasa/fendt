Ultrasonic Testing

Ultrasonic Testing (UT) stands as one of the most prevalent Non-Destructive Testing techniques, and at F AND E NDT (PTY) LTD, we harness its potential to deliver precise and reliable results that matter. UT employs remarkably short ultrasonic pulse-waves to unveil a world of insights within materials.

At F AND E NDT, Ultrasonic Testing is not just a service; it's a commitment to your safety, quality, and success. With our expertise and cutting-edge technology, we bring clarity to the hidden world within your materials, ensuring your peace of mind. Trust us to navigate the depths and deliver results that matter..

Capabilities of Ultrasonic Testing:

  • 1 Detection and Evaluation: UT is your trusted ally in the detection and evaluation of internal defects lurking within materials. We delve deep to ensure your materials meet the highest standards of integrity.
  • 2 Materials Characterization: Understanding your materials is key to success. With UT, we provide comprehensive materials characterization, giving you the insights you need for informed decisions.
  • 3 Thickness Checking and Corrosion Surveys: Thickness matters, especially when it comes to safety and durability. Our UT services include precise thickness checking and corrosion surveys to safeguard your assets.
  • 4 Lamination Testing: In the world of pipes and steel plates, lamination can be a hidden threat. F AND E NDT's UT services bring this threat to light, ensuring the structural integrity of your assets.

How long does an Ultrasonic Testing inspection typically take?

The duration of an Ultrasonic Testing inspection can vary depending on factors such as the size and complexity of the material being tested and the specific requirements of the inspection. F AND E NDT strives to provide efficient and timely inspections while maintaining the highest standards of accuracy.

What types of materials can be tested using Ultrasonic Testing?

Ultrasonic Testing is versatile and can be applied to a wide range of materials, including metals, plastics, composites, and ceramics. It is commonly used in industries such as manufacturing, construction, aerospace, and oil and gas to assess the integrity of various materials and components.
