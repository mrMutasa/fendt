Radiography Testing

At F AND E NDT, we specialize in non-destructive testing methods that go beyond the surface to unveil hidden flaws within materials. Our commitment to excellence in Radiography Testing sets us apart as leaders in the industry. Radiography Testing: This advanced method involves the use of short-wavelength electromagnetic radiation, which consists of high-energy photons, to penetrate various materials. When conducting Radiography Testing, the test piece is carefully positioned between the radiation source and the film (or detector). Through a complex interplay of scattering and absorption processes, the material's density and thickness differences attenuate the penetrating radiation. These variations in absorption are meticulously recorded on film(s).

Radiation Sources: We offer two reliable radioactive sources for industrial use: X-ray and Gamma-ray. These sources utilize higher energy levels, characterized by shorter wavelengths, of electromagnetic waves. It is crucial to emphasize that, due to the radioactivity inherent in radiography testing, strict adherence to Local Rules during operation is of paramount importance to ensure safety and compliance.

Benefits of Radiography Testing:

  • 1 Automatic Detection: It can automatically detect and measure internal flaws. Versatility: Radiography Testing can inspect assembled components, making it suitable for various applications. Comprehensive: It detects both surface and subsurface defects. Permanent Record: Radiography Testing provides a permanent record of the inspection results, aiding in documentation and decision-making processes.
  • 2 Precise Measurement: Radiography Testing allows for measuring dimensions and angles within the sample without the need for sectioning.
  • 3 Sensitivity: It is highly sensitive to changes in thickness, corrosion, flaws, and material density variations.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

If you have any more questions or require further information about our non-destructive testing services, please don't hesitate to reach out to us. We're here to assist you with your NDT needs and provide the necessary guidance and expertise.

1. What is non-destructive testing (NDT), and why is it important?

Non-destructive testing (NDT) is a set of techniques used to inspect materials and components for flaws, defects, or irregularities without causing any damage to the tested item. It's essential because it ensures the safety, reliability, and integrity of critical structures, equipment, and components in various industries, including aerospace, construction, and manufacturing.

2. How long does it typically take to receive the results of an NDT inspection?

The turnaround time for NDT inspection results can vary depending on the complexity of the project, the type of testing used, and the volume of data collected. In general, F AND E NDT strives to provide timely results to our clients to support their decision-making processes and project timelines.

3. Are there any safety concerns associated with radiography testing

Safety is paramount in radiography testing. F AND E NDT strictly adheres to safety protocols and local regulations when handling radioactive sources. Our technicians are highly trained in radiation safety, and we take all necessary precautions to protect both our personnel and the environment during testing operations.
